Overview

QRQ POTA Friday is a casual monthly event for QRQ Crew members who are also POTA Activators. On the last Friday of every month, QC members are encouraged to head out and work the bands — and when conditions allow — push their sending speed a little higher than usual.

This isn't a contest. There are no points and no pressure. It's a way to make POTA activations more interesting, connect with fellow members on the air, and let the POTA community know that high-speed CW is alive and well in the parks.

We're here to create a welcoming, non-judgmental environment where members can develop their abilities comfortably. If that describes you, we'd love to have you as a member of the crew.

How to Participate
As a Member
  1. Activate a POTA park as usual

    Head out to any valid POTA location and set up. No pre-registration is needed — just go activate and enjoy the outdoors.

  2. Push your speed when conditions allow

    When working another QC member or an operator who can copy higher speeds, nudge your speed up. The goal is real-world QRQ practice in a genuine operating environment.

  3. Always be considerate of QRS operators

    POTA is a welcoming community. If a hunter clearly needs you to slow down, do so without hesitation. This event gently encourages speed — it never excludes anyone.

  4. Send your QC member number in the exchange

    When working another QC member, include your QC# in the exchange. This lets members find each other in the logs and sparks curiosity among POTA hunters who may ask about the number.

  5. Spot yourself and listen for other QC members

    Use POTA Spots or QRQ Crew channels to let other members know you're on the air. A quick CQ POTA QC alongside your standard CQ POTA goes a long way.

Suggested Exchange Format
RST   QC#   NAME   QTH
Example:  599 QC007 PETE TX  ·  Your member number is the key piece — always include it when working another QC member.
As a Hunter
  1. Push your CW speed as high as you're comfortable

    When you work a QC member during a POTA activation, take the opportunity to stretch your sending speed. There's no obligation to go faster than feels natural — but this is a great moment to challenge yourself and see what you're capable of. Even a small step up from your usual pace is worth celebrating.

  2. Ask about membership if you're up for it

    If you find yourself comfortably holding the exchange at 35 WPM and like what you hear, ask the activating member about joining the QRQ Crew. Membership requires a 35 WPM QSO with a current member — if you just pulled one off, you may already be halfway there.

Spirit of the Event

QRQ POTA Friday sits at the intersection of two great amateur radio traditions: the joy of operating portable from beautiful outdoor locations, and the artistry of high-speed CW. Above all, this event is meant to spark curiosity — when a POTA hunter hears a crisp 40 WPM signal and sees a QC# in the log, they might just look it up. That's how the QRQ Crew grows, one fast exchange at a time.
